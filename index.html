Traffic Simulator

Interactive implementations of mathematical traffic-flow models — both macroscopic continuum models governed by partial differential equations, and microscopic car-following models that simulate individual vehicles. Built in the browser with vector graphics and real physical units.

Macroscopic Models — continuum partial differential equations

Macro 1st Order

LWR — Lighthill–Whitham–Richards Kinematic Wave

First-order macroscopic model in which traffic density evolves as a scalar conservation law. Vehicle speed is determined entirely by local density via a Greenshields equilibrium relation — no driver anticipation. Godunov finite-volume scheme on a ring road. Includes a density heatmap, travel-time heatmap, and passive micro-car tracer visualisation.

∂ρ/∂t + ∂(ρ · v_e(ρ))/∂x = 0
Macro 2nd Order

ARZ — Aw–Rascle–Zhang Second-Order Model

Second-order macroscopic model that adds a driver-anticipation pressure term, correcting the wrong-way traffic instability present in the earlier Payne–Whitham model. Solved with a Rusanov (local Lax–Friedrichs) finite-volume scheme and operator-split relaxation toward equilibrium speed. Includes density and travel-time heatmaps with passive micro-car tracer visualisation.

∂ρ/∂t + ∂(ρv)/∂x = 0
∂y/∂t + ∂(yv)/∂x = ρ(v_e(ρ) − v) / τ

Microscopic Models — individual vehicles ★ most interactive

Micro

OVM / IDM — Car-Following

The richest simulator on this page. Each vehicle is simulated individually using either the Optimal Velocity Model (Bando et al., 1995) or the Intelligent Driver Model (Treiber et al., 2000). Vehicles react to the gap and relative speed of the car ahead.

Features multi-lane roads with MOBIL lane-changing, draggable obstacles (cones, barriers, traffic lights), configurable per-lane inflow, and five road configurations: ring road, circular ring road, straight road, straight road with on-ramp, and straight road with off-ramp.

Note: when switching road configuration, press Restart to apply the change.
